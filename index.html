The Cost Nobody Tracks

The invisible maintenance tax

Most facilities don't have a maintenance crisis. They have a maintenance blind spot — and it's expensive in ways that never show up as one clean line item.

Reactive, Not Preventive

You pay for the breakdown, not the fix.

Without a scheduled system, equipment gets serviced after it fails — the most expensive time to service anything. Preventable failures become emergency repairs.

Compliance Exposure

Nobody can produce the paper trail.

When an inspector, insurer, or new tenant asks for maintenance history and compliance documents, "we think we did that" is not an answer they accept.

No Accountability

Nobody owns the asset's condition.

Without a register and an audit trail, there's no way to know who serviced what, when, or whether it was done to standard — until something fails.

This is fixable with one system — not a bigger maintenance team.
